Q : What do you think about the role of internship experience in the application? Do you have any suggestions for undergraduates while looking for internships?

A : Internship experiences are part of the shiniest points on your resume as a fresh graduate student. Recruiters like to look at your internship company and project outcomes to evaluate your skill sets and background match. Internship experiences are an acknowledgment from related industries and will help you on job hunting. For undergraduates who are seeking internships, my recommendation is to contact your schoolâ€™s career help center to properly format and prepare your official resume and plan for couple versions if you decide to seek internships in different academic areas. Your research/lecture professors usually have good connections with their industries and maintaining a good relationship with them such as to earn a remarkable grade in their courses or become a member of his research team will expose your strengths and attitudes, which may lead to a good result that you may be introduced to company recruiters/managers directly from your professors. Besides proper preparation for interviews, you should also think about company reputation, industrial/social connections while choosing offers.
